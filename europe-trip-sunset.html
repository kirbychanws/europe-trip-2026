London

United Kingdom · Days 1–4

3–4 days recommended
Top Highlights
👑

Tower of London

Crown Jewels and 1,000 years of history. Book ahead — sells out fast.

🏰

Buckingham Palace

Changing of the Guard is free. Arrive early for a good spot.

🏛

British Museum

Free world-class museum. Rosetta Stone, Egyptian mummies, Greek treasures.

🍜

Borough Market

London's best food market near London Bridge. Open Thu–Sat.

🌸

Notting Hill

Colourful streets and Portobello Road Market. Best on Saturdays.

🎨

Tate Modern

Free contemporary art museum with stunning Thames views from the top.

🌳

Hyde Park

Massive royal park — perfect for a May afternoon picnic.

🎸

Camden Market

Eclectic street food, fashion, live music. Very lively on weekends.

Hotel Notes
Book Tower of London and popular restaurants well in advance — May is busy season in London.
🛺

Lisbon

Portugal · Days 5–8

3–4 days recommended
Top Highlights
🏘

Alfama District

Oldest neighbourhood — fado music, cobblestone alleys, hilltop views.

🗼

Belém Tower

16th-century UNESCO fortress on the Tagus River. Book ahead.

⛪

Jerónimos Monastery

Stunning Manueline architecture. Houses Vasco da Gama's tomb.

🥐

Pastéis de Belém

The original custard tart bakery since 1837. Always worth the queue.

🌅

Miradouros

Scenic viewpoints. Senhora do Monte has the best sunset panorama.

🚃

Tram 28

Historic yellow tram through Alfama. Go early morning to beat crowds.

🛍

LX Factory

Industrial creative hub with great shops and Sunday market.

🍽

Time Out Market

Famous food hall — try 6–8 Portuguese dishes in one sitting.

Hotel Notes
Lisbon is one of Europe's best value capitals. Excellent wine and food at a fraction of Paris prices. May weather is perfect — around 22°C with long sunny days.
🧇

Brussels

Belgium · Days 9–10

2 days recommended
Top Highlights
🏛

Grand Place

One of Europe's most beautiful squares. Magical at night with the lights.

👦

Manneken Pis

The famous tiny statue — smaller than expected but a Brussels institution.

🎨

Magritte Museum

Dedicated to Belgian surrealist René Magritte. Fantastic collection.

🧇

Belgian Waffles

Get a warm Liège waffle from a street vendor. Genuinely life-changing.

🍫

Chocolate Shops

Neuhaus, Godiva and dozens of artisan chocolatiers on every block.

☕

Ixelles Neighbourhood

Bohemian area with great cafes and Art Nouveau architecture.

Always take the train from Brussels to Paris — faster than flying once you include airport time, and drops you right in the city centre.

Paris

France · Days 11–14

3–4 days recommended
Top Highlights
✨

Eiffel Tower

Book summit tickets weeks ahead. Go at dusk then stay for the night sparkle show.

🖼

The Louvre

World's largest art museum. Pre-book — queues without tickets can be 2–3 hours.

🌸

Musée d'Orsay

Best Impressionist collection in the world. Monet, Renoir, Van Gogh.

⛪

Notre Dame

Newly reopened after renovation — more beautiful than ever. Go early.

🎨

Montmartre

Hilltop artist's quarter with Sacré-Cœur and stunning city views.

🛍

Le Marais

Trendy neighbourhood with galleries, boutiques and great cafes.

👑

Palace of Versailles

Day trip — 40 min by RER C. The Hall of Mirrors and gardens are extraordinary.

🎾

French Open

Roland Garros tennis starts May 24 — book if you're a fan!

Hotel Notes
Pre-book everything in Paris — Eiffel Tower, Louvre, and Musée d'Orsay all sell out weeks ahead in May. Centre Pompidou is closed for renovation 2026–2030.
🚲

Copenhagen

Denmark · Days 15–17

3 days recommended
Top Highlights
🌈

Nyhavn

Iconic colourful harbour. Best enjoyed with a cold beer waterside.

🎡

Tivoli Gardens

Historic amusement park in the city. Magical when the evening lights come on.

🏰

Rosenborg Castle

Renaissance castle with Crown Jewels. Beautiful King's Garden surrounds it.

🧜

The Little Mermaid

Iconic but small — see it but manage your expectations.

🏘

Freetown Christiania

Unique self-governed neighbourhood since 1971. Unlike anywhere else.

🚲

Cycling

Rent a bike — Copenhagen is flat with excellent dedicated bike lanes.

🥪

Smørrebrød

Traditional open-faced sandwiches — must try for lunch at Aamanns.

👑

Amalienborg Palace

Royal residence. Changing of the guard at noon daily.

Hotel Notes
Copenhagen is expensive — budget around €25–35 for a main course. But the food quality is extraordinary. Cycling is the cheapest and most enjoyable way to explore.
